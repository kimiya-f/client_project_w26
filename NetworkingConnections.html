Networking and Building Connections

Why Networking Matters

Networking is an essential part of career growth. Building meaningful professional relationships can help you learn about different career paths, access opportunities, gain advice, and develop long-term connections in your field.

Whether you are attending career fairs, reaching out to alumni, joining student organizations, or connecting with professionals online, having a strategy can help you make the most of your networking efforts.

Planning Your Networking Strategy

  • Set Your Goals: Identify what you want to achieve through networking, such as learning about a specific industry, finding mentors, or discovering internship opportunities.
  • Identify Contacts: Consider alumni, professors, peers, and professionals in your field. Make a list of people and organizations to reach out to.
  • Engage in Events: Attend career fairs, informational sessions, conferences, and online events to meet new people.
  • Follow Up: Keep in touch with contacts after meetings or events. Thank them for their time and maintain a professional relationship.
